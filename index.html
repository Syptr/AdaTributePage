Ada King, Countess of Lovelace

The first computer programmer - a hero for women and pioneer for the world.

Some Interesting Facts

  • Born: December 10, 1815, London, United Kingdom as Augusta Ada Byron
  • She was a Mathematician.
  • She was an associate of Charles Babbage and his work on the Analytical Engine. In her work she explained that the machine could do more than calculate - it could follow patterns or codes.
  • Died: November 27, 1852, Marylebone, London, United Kingdom
  • The first recognition for her work would come a century after her passing.
  • In the 1970's, the US Department of Defense developed the programming language "Ada" in her honor.
